# SLUDGE: Sequenced Lineages for Understanding Digester Genomes for Engineering

## SLUDGE is a curated database of high and medium quality metagenome-assembled genomes (MAGs) recovered from anaerobic digesters and related bioreactor systems. SLUDGE is a resouce for studying the microbial communities underlying biogas and volatile fatty acid production for understanding microbial communities underlying waste treatment and resource recovery.

By integrating MAGs from diverse sources and standardizing their annotation, SLUDGE enables:

Comparative genomics across digester ecosystems

Metabolic pathway reconstruction for key microbial lineages

Community function modeling to inform process optimization and engineering design

üß¨ Why SLUDGE?

Anaerobic digesters are complex, microbially-driven ecosystems central to sustainable waste management and bioenergy. Yet, the diversity and function of many lineages remain poorly understood. SLUDGE provides a foundation for linking genome-level data to system performance, accelerating both basic and applied research in environmental biotechnology.
